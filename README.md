## What This Case Is About

Nadia Zahmoul is an autistic litigant in person with diagnosed PTSD, managing four parallel High Court proceedings in England and Wales without legal representation since March 2024.

This archive documents a single pattern across all proceedings: **every reviewing body has engaged with a substituted or mischaracterised version of the claim actually pleaded.** Each adverse decision creates a history that subsequent courts read as evidence of lack of merit — rather than evidence that the claim was never properly determined.

**The binary question at the centre of all proceedings:**

> Does an identifiable judicial order revoking Mrs Zahmoul's statutory participation measures under FPR Part 3A exist — or does it not?

No institution has answered this question. Not the Family Division. Not the Court of Appeal. Not the Administrative Court. Not the Justice Committee. Not the Lord Chancellor. Not HMCTS. Not the Metropolitan Police.

## The Substitution Pattern

Across all proceedings, the same pattern repeats:

1. A claim is filed with specific, identified grounds.
2. The reviewing body engages with a simplified or mischaracterised version of the claim.
3. The claim is refused on the basis of that substituted version.
4. The refusal is then cited by the next reviewing body as evidence that the claim lacks merit.
5. The actual grounds — as pleaded — remain undetermined.

This is not a disagreement about law. It is a question of whether the claim was ever determined at all.
